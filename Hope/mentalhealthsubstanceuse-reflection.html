Understanding Self-Medication

Explain what "self-medication" means and why it ultimately makes mental health symptoms worse rather than better. Use a specific example from the reading.

💡 Hint: Think about a specific pairing — like anxiety and alcohol — and trace what happens to symptoms over time, not just in the moment.

2

Co-Occurring Conditions

Choose one co-occurring condition from Section 3 — depression, anxiety, ADHD, trauma, or chronic stress. Explain why teens with that condition might be more likely to use substances, and what would actually help them more than substances would.

💡 Hint: Use what you learned about both that condition and the healthy coping strategies in Section 4.

3

Applying Coping Strategies

Of the healthy coping strategies listed in Section 4, which two do you think would be most useful in your own life and why? Be specific about what situations you might use them in.

💡 Hint: Think about stress, pressure, or difficult emotions you actually experience — not just hypothetical ones.

4

Risk Factor vs. Destiny

The reading states that having a mental health condition is a "risk factor, not destiny." What does this mean? How does understanding this change the way we should think about and treat people who struggle with both mental health and substance use?

💡 Hint: Think about stigma, judgment, and empathy — and what it looks like to respond with understanding instead of criticism.
